Habit Tracker

I developed this app during the coronavirus quarantine in March 2020, in an effort to develop a routine and schedule, but to simultaneously not put too much pressure on myself (and users) while they coped with the quarantine. I wanted to create an app that lets you set tasks that you ideally want to do every day– but doesn't pressure you to do them at any particular time. With this concept in mind, I developed an app that lets you add habits and log your consistency of doing them every day of the week– and lets you start afresh with a clean slate every new week. The timespan of a week to track your habits was the ideal middle ground between taking it one day at a time, and giving yourself a flexible routine. Click here for a link to the live site, here for the Github repo for the frontend, and here for the Github repo for the backend

  • Backend: NodeJS, Express, RESTful APIs, Helmet, XSS, Morgan, Postgres, PSQL, DBeaver, CORS, knex, Chai

Audition Tracker

As an actor, I am all too familiar with the panic of having to keep track of my auditions– who called me in, what role it was for, what I wore, how I thought it went, and so on. These details may seem minor to a non-actor, but they are vital to a working actor– new industry connections and relationships can sometimes be more important than booking a part. I made the Audition Tracker to have this record of auditions and connections you make as a working actor in New York City, that you can refer to in case an agent or casting director wants to know about your recent audition history.
